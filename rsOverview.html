Demonstration of the jQuery Overview plug-in

What it is?

This plug-in provides an overview of the whole page to the user, by reacting to changes in the horizontal, vertical or both scrolling positions, as well the viewport and content size.
It renders two rectangles, one inside another. The darkest one represents the viewport area (your browser window).
The lightest rectangle, represents the entire document area.
This allows anyone to quickly jump to any position within the document, bookmark several locations and visit them later on.

It provides methods to load/save all bookmarks between page transitions, so that your users can get their bookmarks back everytime they return to an old page.

Also works on overflowed elements and is an alternative to implement your own scroll bars, when 'direction' parameter is either horizontal or vertical.

It is very lightweight and highly customizable through CSS.

How it works?

This page contains two instances of the Overview plug-in:
  1. On the top right corner, you can a red/orange instance that monitors the content of this entire page.
    It should have a fixed position, to prevent it from moving out of sight when the user navigates through the page.
  2. Below, another blue/cyan instance that monitors the overflowed element located right next to it.

To get a felling of what is this all about, please do the following:

  • Basic stuff
    • Drag and move the viewport (the small darker rectangle)
    • Click on the big rectangle for fast scroll
    • Keep an eye on the red/orange instance, while you scroll and resize your browser window.
    • Click on the add/remove content buttons and watch how the plug-in reacts to changes in content size.
  • Advanced (bookmarks)
    • Click on the Toggle bookmark, to save/clear the current position.
    • Scroll to a different location and create another bookmark. Now click on the prev/next buttons
    • Click on the small bookmark squares
